# Five-Year Multi-Path Life Planning

## Prompt

Guide me through a structured multi-path life-planning exercise. Use my current career direction, abilities, constraints, responsibilities, values, and genuine interests to design three meaningfully different life paths and explore how each could develop over the next five years.

Do not pretend to predict the future. Treat every projection as a scenario based on stated assumptions. Point out missing information, uncertainty, tradeoffs, and factors outside my control.

## Start by Gathering Context

Ask only the most decision-relevant questions about:

- Current work, skills, finances, and obligations
- Activities that energize or drain me
- Preferred lifestyle, location, pace, and relationships
- Risk tolerance and minimum acceptable stability
- Values I do not want to compromise
- Opportunities I am already considering

## Scenario 1: Optimize the Current Path

Assume I make no major directional change but execute my current commitments exceptionally well. Describe who I may become in five years, including likely skills, reputation, lifestyle, income range assumptions, satisfaction, risks, and opportunity costs.

## Scenario 2: Industry Disruption and Reinvention

Assume my current industry rapidly declines or disappears. Identify substantially different fields I could enter using transferable skills, temperament, interests, and available learning time. Explain the bridge from where I am now to each credible alternative.

## Scenario 3: Freedom from Money and Social Approval

Assume I have financial independence and no need to satisfy other people’s expectations. Explore the life I would most want to build: how I spend my time, where and how I live, what I create or contribute, and which relationships and routines matter most. Distinguish a durable preference from a temporary fantasy.

## Five-Year Simulation

For each path, provide:

1. A short path name and defining idea
2. The assumptions behind it
3. A year-by-year outline for Years 1–5
4. Skills, relationships, resources, and habits gained or lost
5. Best case, base case, and failure mode
6. Early warning signs and decision checkpoints
7. Reversible experiments I can run before committing

## Evaluation

Score each path from 1–10 on:

- Alignment with values
- Meaning and enjoyment
- Financial resilience
- Feasibility
- Growth potential
- Lifestyle fit
- Reversibility
- Downside risk

Explain the scores and do not manipulate the weighting to force a preferred answer. Finish with one concrete action I can complete within seven days to move toward the most attractive future or test whether it truly fits me.

## Expected Output

Provide a comparison table, three five-year timelines, a risk-and-assumption register, a scoring matrix, and one immediate next step.
