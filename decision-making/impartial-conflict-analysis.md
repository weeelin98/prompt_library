# Impartial Conflict Analysis

## Prompt

Help me analyze a real-life disagreement as impartially as possible.

I will describe the situation using neutral labels such as **Person A** and **Person B**, rather than identifying which person is me. Unless gender, sexual orientation, age, ethnicity, or another personal characteristic is directly relevant to the conflict, omit it. Base your analysis on the reported actions, words, expectations, boundaries, consequences, and available evidence—not on identity-based assumptions.

Follow this structure:

1. **Separate facts from interpretations.** List what was directly observed or said, then list assumptions, inferred motives, and missing information.
2. **Steelman both perspectives.** Present the strongest reasonable interpretation of Person A’s position and Person B’s position.
3. **Assess conduct consistently.** Apply the same standards to both people. Consider proportionality, honesty, consent, reciprocity, responsibility, boundary-setting, and whether expectations were communicated.
4. **Identify uncertainty.** Explain which conclusions could change if missing facts were supplied. Do not diagnose either person or claim to know hidden motives.
5. **Evaluate responsibility.** State where each person acted reasonably, poorly, or ambiguously. Do not force a 50/50 conclusion if the evidence is asymmetric.
6. **Recommend next steps.** Suggest specific, realistic actions, including a sample message when useful. Prioritize safety and distance if the situation involves threats, coercion, stalking, or abuse.

Avoid flattering the narrator, automatically validating either side, or reducing the situation to “who is good and who is bad.” The goal is a fair assessment and a workable response.
